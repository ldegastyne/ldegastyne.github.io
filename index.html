Who I Am:

I am a Sociology major (with a focus in Social Inequality) and a Professional and Technical Writing minor. I chose Sociology because I am passionate about helping people, and I like to learn about why they do what they do in various contexts and situations. I'm not interested in the prescribed career paths of social work or research but I think the skills I've learned in the major can apply to a wide variety of occupations and will assist me in dealing with people in whatever field I choose. I hope to get a job more along the lines of my minor, editing or writing in a magazine publication promoting or discussing a cause I care about. I care about a number of issues and causes, including improving race relations in the United States, increasing local church involvement of college students in the United States, combatting sex trafficking around the world, and raising awareness about eating disorders in adolescent girls (and the positive resources available to them). I think I will focus on the fourth cause for the project in this class. Right now I don't have any real concerns about this course. I don't think of myself as a "computer person" but I will need these skills in my career field so I am excited to learn. Specifically I am interested in learning about digital graphic design but general HTML and CSS knowledge will also be extremely helpful.

What My Project Is:

I chose this issue because I personally dealt with it in high school and early college but as I was recovering I recognized that most people don't understand the signs, symptoms, and irreparable damages of an eating disorder. I saw that many of the girls who are experiencing it are too afraid to speak up about it, and this results in them not getting help and therefore letting it get even worse. My audience for awareness is the general public because the things that families, friends, teachers, etc. say to affected girls have a huge effect on how likely they are to seek out help. If more people know what an eating disorder looks like and what some of the causes and triggers are for it they can help to prevent it. In my audience I am also targeting the girls themseles who have anorexia nervosa, bulimia, unclassified eating disorder, etc. (diagnosed or not) because I want them to know what options are available to them to recover. A specific organization who does a lot of this work and has a lot of these same goals is NEDA, or the National Eating Disorder Association.
